Why Franchise Owners Fail at Online Marketing

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2022
  • 12 Comments

While owning a franchise comes with established brand recognition and support, many franchise owners grapple with local growth. Simply being part of a recognizable brand does little for a franchise, in fact, franchise owners often find their online marketing efforts to be dissatisfying in lead generation capability. Franchise SEO, lack of effective digital strategies, and very little personalization makes the gaps much wider. Customers today do plenty of research on franchises, by the time they walk into your store or franchise, they have Googled about you, searched you on social, and/or read reviews on aggregate platforms. Without effective digital marketing for franchise businesses, owners may lose local market visibility to competitors. We want to take a look at why franchise marketing is not effective online, and ways to fix the problem.

Why Online Marketing Matters for Franchise Owners?

Franchise growth in today’s digital-first world can’t depend only on the brand name. Every franchise needs their own specific franchise digital marketing strategy due to local competition, changing consumer behaviors, and increased dependence on online reviews. A solid franchise SEO strategy secures brand visibility in local searches. Google Ads for Franchises drive leads quickly. Social platforms create connections to build community around your location and create loyalty for your customers, so when you don’t have or implement these strategies, franchise owners can have a hard time generating local growth.

Mistakes Franchise Owners Make in Online Marketing

Here are the typical reasons why franchise digital marketing misses:

  • Over-relying on brand name: They assume that their customers will come just on the basis of the franchise name.
  • Local SEO for franchises not ranking: When the franchise will not appear for searches like "pizza outlet near me" or "franchise location in [city]."
  • Generic posts: When they copy and paste posts that the brand uses without tailoring it for the local clientele.
  • Ignoring reviews: When they ignore Google star rating and reviews, it can cause problems with credibility.
  • Poor ad targeting: When ads are run with no regard to the local customer base, it creates a budget drain.

When ads are run with no regard to the local customer base, it creates a budget drain.

How to Fix Franchise Marketing Gaps

To be effective online, franchise owners should combine brand guidelines with localized strategies. Here’s how:

  • Invest in franchise SEO: Optimize organic search for hyperlocal keywords, such as “best gym franchise in [city]”.
  • Run Google Ads for franchises: Focus on targeting prospects searching for services/products within your vicinity.
  • Localize contentProviding local event, offer, and community-type content connects with a nearby audience.
  • Social media storytelling: Share behind-the-scenes stories about local staff and franchise customer engagement experiences.
  • Reputation management: Ask happy customers to provide a review, and quickly address any negative reviews.
  • WhatsApp campaigns for franchises: Directly promote offers and send reminders to local customers.

If done well, your franchise strategies will reposition your business as a neighborhood favorite rather than just another generic franchise shop.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why do most franchise businesses fail at online marketing?

They rely too much on the core brand and don’t engage in local SEO for franchise owners, reviews, or campaigns that are personalized.

Can a franchise businessman market their business on their own?

Yes! With a good franchise digital marketing strategy, these owners can create visibility in their local area while still complying with brand standards.

What’s the best online marketing channel for franchises?

The ideal marketing mix consists of local SEO for franchises, Google Ads for franchises, and franchise social media strategy.

Do reviews mean anything for franchise businesses?

Yes, reviews are a big deal. Positive Google reviews can go a long way in your local area for increasing trust and customers.

How much should franchise owners spend on online marketing?

In general they want to spend 5–10% of their monthly revenue in franchise digital marketing for consistent growth.
