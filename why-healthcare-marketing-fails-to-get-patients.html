Why Healthcare Marketing Fails to Get Patients

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2022
  • 12 Comments

In today's competitive healthcare market, drawing in patients is increasingly more difficult. Many hospitals, clinics, and healthcare providers undertake some sort of campaigns, but the result is often disappointing. Why? This is due to ineffective or incomplete healthcare marketing. Ranging from ineffective healthcare SEO (Search Engine Optimization) to a lack of social media interaction, something is preventing the healthcare facility from engaging the right audience. Given that patients today rely primarily on Google searches and online reviews to select their doctors or hospitals, without a good healthcare digital marketing strategy, these healthcare businesses will lose business to their competitors. With this blog, we will explore why healthcare marketing fails, and how to remedy the issue.

Why Healthcare Digital Marketing Is Crucial?

Patients no longer sift through directories in search of hospitals and clinics; they search online. A clinic that does not engage in healthcare digital marketing is putting itself in a precarious situation because they may never find you. A robust SEO for healthcare websites plan will push your hospital website up in the rankings in search engine results pages (SERPs) while Google Ads for healthcare will place your hospital in front of potential patients almost immediately. Social media marketing for healthcare —Instagram and Facebook—can also play a role in garnering interest from a wide variety of audiences (especially younger patients) who seek social proof before making a healthcare choice. If you do not have the tactics talked about in the previous two sentences, then your business won't create interest from patients and never create that continuum of opportunity for inquiry.

Common Mistakes That Kill Healthcare Marketing

If you’re not generating additional patients through your healthcare marketing campaigns, it’s likely that one or more of the following mistakes is the culprit:

  • No clear digital presence: When a healthcare organization lacks a website or has an outdated website, it decreases potential patient trust.
  • Weak healthcare SEO: If your organization doesn’t rank for what prospective patients would search for (like “dentist near me” or “best hospital in [city]”), they can’t find you.
  • Generic healthcare content marketing: If your organization shares posts but doesn’t provide any patient-related information, your organization’s marketing efforts (even with branding) will not be seen, or even worse, be dismissed.
  • Lack of online reputation management for healthcare: If your organization ignores reviews or has negative reviews, you will likely appear less credible to a prospective patient.
  • No targeted ads: Generic ads on Facebook or Google that don't talk specifically about patient-related conditions, needs or pain points will lead to wasted dollars.

The above mistakes result in a trust disconnect for patients, as they can’t find a trusted healthcare organization after searching on Google or Facebook, and will likely seek an alternative healthcare organization.

How to Fix Your Healthcare Marketing Strategy?

In order to consistently draw in patients, it is necessary for healthcare practices to reassess their approach to digital health marketing:

  • Establish a healthcare SEO strategy: This strategy utilizes keywords that patients would be searching for like, “orthopedic clinic in Mumbai,” or “child specialist near me.”
  • Leverage Google Ads for healthcare: Using Google Ads, you can capture patients when they are actively searching for your service with high intent ads.
  • Engage online with healthcare social media marketing strategy: Post patient testimonials, healthcare tips or conduct live Q&As to create rapport and trust.
  • Explore healthcare content marketing: Blogging, video creation, and infographics that answer common health questions draw patients to practices organically.
  • Add WhatsApp campaigns for healthcare: Sending appointment confirmations on WhatsApp is faster for patients than traditional texts or emails, and you can also send reminders for patients easier than with a phone call.
  • Maintain an online reputation management for hospitals strategy: Encouraging patients who had an excellent experience to leave a positive review for your practice on Google and/or healthcare specific directories will help you manage your online reputation.

Following these recommendations, hospitals and clinics will improve patient referral rates through rapport, trust, and patient engagement; and hospitals and clinics can ensure patients continue to book appointments in a timely manner.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why isn’t my healthcare marketing getting patients?

In part due to weak healthcare SEO, poor online reputation management for healthcare, and lack of patient-focused healthcare content marketing, many healthcare campaigns fail to convert.

Is digital marketing necessary for small clinics?

Yes. Even smaller clinics can squash their competition and drive patient bookings in a cost-effective way by implementing local SEO for healthcare and WhatsApp campaigns for healthcare.

Which digital channel is best for growing patient load?

A combination of Google Ads for healthcare, SEO for hospitals, and healthcare social media marketing strategy, and ideally a website, is the best approach for attracting new patients.

Can social media really help in healthcare?

Yes, by sharing health tips, testimonials, interactive posts, and healthcare news, healthcare providers can establish their trust and visibility through social media marketing for healthcare.

How much of my revenue should healthcare businesses invest in marketing?

Experts recommend that healthcare businesses invest around 5-8% of all revenue into digital marketing for healthcare for the long term, in order to grow their patient base.
